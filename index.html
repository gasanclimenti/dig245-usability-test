(Un)usability

A usability test on sports website www.espn.com for their user-friendless, organization, and accessibility.


Looking for Scores

Task: Find the scores from the most recent Carolina Panthers football game.

Location: Scores can be found on the NFL tab or the top of the home screen (they're harder to find if the Panthers lose and the font is colored in gray!).

Biggest plus: There are many different directions to find the same results.

Biggest challenge: The scores were often skipped over by our tester, as the font appeared in a difficult-to-spot light gray.

Time taken: slow (2-3 minutes)

Task rating: unecessarily difficult

Result: successful, but timely


Listening to the Radio

Task: Locate and listen to a live ESPN radio show.

Location: First, select the “Listen” option on the top menu of the home page, then select “Listen Live” or “ESPN Radio.”

Biggest plus: It only took a few clicks to get to the right place!

Biggest challenge: The ESPN radio page offers an unorganized mix of live and non-live stations.

Time taken: quick (30-60 seconds)

Task rating: efficient, simple, easy

Result: successful!


Creating a Fantasy Account

Task: Try to sign up for a fantasy league of any sport.

Location: If fantasy leagues are not featured on the home page, as it was in the test, select the “Fantasy” option on the top menu of the home page, then choose a specific sport.

Biggest plus: Joining a league is a simple process and the page is well organized, with explantory buttons.

Biggest challenge: An ESPN account is necessary to sign up.

Time taken: quick (30-60 seconds)

Task rating: easy, especially when fantasy leagues are advertised on the home page

Result: successful!


Checking out Little League

Task: Find the final scores from the most recent Little League World Series.

Location: The Little League World Seires, among other sports, can be found by clicking the three white dots on the top menu of the home page.

Biggest plus: ESPN provides scores for less-maintstream sports like Little League.

Biggest challenge: With so many sports on ESPN's page, Little League can be hard to find; the scores were even more hidden.

Time taken: slow (2-3 minutes)

Task rating: tricky!

Result: successful, but timely


Watching a Video

Task: Watch a related video and turn on captions (accessibility test).

Location: Videos covering all different sports are posted throughout the home page, as well as under the "Watch" option on the top menu.

Biggest plus: Videos are provided to showcase highlights in a visual format other than articles.

Biggest challenge: Not all videos, like the one the tester found, provided close captioning (unaccessible).

Time taken: normal (1-2 minutes)

Task rating: unaccessible!

Result: somewhat successfull (fault of the site)


Recommendations to ESPN

Add more whitespace to prevent overcrowding information on their home page.

Organize content in a more minimal way, as to not overwhelm users when they open the site.

Provide captions for all videos to make them accesible to users with hearing impairments.
